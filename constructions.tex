\paragraph{IV-based PK-AEAD in the ROM. }
In Figure~\ref{fig:ro-kem-dem-construction} we give a simple construction of an IV-based PK-AEAD scheme that is secure  in the random-oracle model. \task{Prove! Actually, you can't until you specify what $\mdalg$ is, or at least some security-relevant assumptions on it.  Also going to need to sort out what security notion(s) the KEM scheme needs to meet.}  Operationally, it works for any randomized encapsulation algorithm $\OLencapprim{\pk}{}$, replacing its internally generated randomness with the output of the random oracle.  For example, the standard El Gamal KEM where $K=g^{\overline{S}x}$ and $X=g^{\overline{S}}$, for a generator~$g$ and $(\pk,\sk)=(g^x,x)$.
